Yuval Medina is a composer and pianist based in New York City, working at the intersection of music with dance, film, and the concert stage. At the core of his work is a commitment to storytelling, immersive experience, and the inherently human power of music — its ability to bridge the widest divides and remind us we are in this together.

His recent projects reflect this vision across scales: from Together Again (2025), a sold-out evening-length multimedia production featuring original works for chamber ensemble, dance, and film, which he composed, produced, and directed; to The Long Goodbye (2026), a fifteen-minute brass ensemble work commissioned by the Manhattan School of Music and conducted by R. John Sheppard of the American Ballet Theatre and American Symphony Orchestra; to his orchestral rescore of BBC's Frozen Planet II, which has been viewed over 2.8 million times on YouTube. His music was featured by Vanity Fair during the 2025 Oscars ceremony, accompanying stars including Adrien Brody, Olivia Wilde, and Andrew Garfield, in collaboration with photographer Álvaro Colóm.

Yuval is a co-founder of Ex Corde, a composer-led nonprofit presenting curated programs of contemporary composers alongside timeless masterpieces of the orchestral, chamber, and choral repertoire. Beyond Ex Corde, Yuval's broader mission as a composer is to challenge the conventions of the classical world while resisting the alienation of contemporary culture — creating, through music and through collaborations across dance, film, and theater, spaces for genuine connection and shared humanity. He is currently developing a large-scale immersive work combining orchestral music with dance, theater, animation, and lighting. A preview of this project, Meteors, will be performed by the Manhattan School of Music orchestra on April 10th, 2026 — the first chapter of an evening-length work he intends to bring to the world's major stages.

Born in Israel and raised in the United States, Yuval immigrated at age eleven with limited English and found in music a universal language through which he could be understood. As a gay artist who grew up closeted, composing became a vital means of emotional expression in moments when words failed. These early experiences continue to shape his artistic voice — a voice that seeks not only beauty, but truth, urgency, and connection.

After earning a B.S. in Computer Science from Duke University, Yuval worked as a software engineer at Google before making the decision to pursue composition full-time. He is completing his M.M. in Composition at the Manhattan School of Music. He has received awards from Tribeca New Music, the New York Youth Symphony, and the American Pops Orchestra, where he was selected as a finalist in the 2026 Ruth Wales du Pont Collegiate Composition Competition.

You can follow @yuvalcomposer on Instagram, YouTube, or SoundCloud to stay up to date with his latest work.
